E-TREASURE HUNT

Event type :  Technical
Participation: : Open to ECE, EEE, and BME Departments (2-3 in a team)
Pre-requisites: Basics of Electronics, Circuit Theory, Boolean Algebra, Reliable network connection.
Venue: Google Forms, Zoom & an online gaming platform

Event Head:
• Divya Seshadri M- 7338973878
• Vignesh S-  90924 89776
• Shwetha S-  9790950184


Round 1
Duration:  15 mins
● Round 1 is a technical Quiz conducted using google forms.
● The quiz comprises 15 questions based on 1st and 2nd year ECE syllabus.
● Quiz questions are to be prepared in collaboration with the QUIZ CLUB.
● Of this, 4 or 5 teams (depending on the number of participants) will be qualified for Round 2.
Rules:
● Every participant registered for this event should have a Team leader.
● 5 minutes before the scheduled start time of this round, every team leader will receive a Google form link to his/her registered email ID.
● The team members can connect through zoom or meet or any means to communicate among each other.
● There will be no proctoring.
● Google form can be accessed only by the team leader through the registered email ID.
● Duration of this round is 30 minutes.
● Within this time, he/she should complete the quiz and submit it. No extra time will be given. There will be negative markings in this round.
● In case of ties, faster they finish they will get an advantage in the rankings.


Round 2
Duration: 45 mins
● The participants qualified for the second round will be given clues regarding circuit
components. Participants will have to decode / crack the clues and identify the components.
● They will also be given a partially constructed circuit which will be hidden in a website. Clues will be given to find the website as well.
● Once both the components and circuit are found, participants are expected to complete the circuit and simulate it.
Rules:
● All the participants will receive a zoom meeting link to the registered email ID.
● The team leader is expected to share the output simulation screen to the organizer.
● Duration of this round is 45 mins.
● The fastest team to submit the correct output will be qualified.



Round 3 (Finals)
Duration: 60 mins
The participants qualified for Finals will be called for an online game based on 2nd year ECE Syllabus.
Game Concept:
● In this GUI game they will be provided with p and n type semiconductors.
● The participants should be able to design a given circuit and show its desired output.
● The basic circuits like dual input AND and OR gates, Flip Flops, Adders, Counters andmultiplexers will be asked.
Rules:
● All the participants will receive a zoom meeting link and an online game link to theregistered email ID.
● The duration of this round is 60 minutes.
● The participants can verify their output by using a verification tab present on the samescreen where they will be designing the circuit.
● After successful completion of this game, participants are expected to show the outputby sharing the screen to the organizer present in the meeting.
● The team which shows the output first will be declared as the winner.
● If the answer is yet to be found even after 30 minutes, clues can be given until one ofthe teams finds the correct answer.

Rules:
1. All registered teams will choose a theme from a given list of options based on Core
technology prior to the event day.
2. From chosen theme, they should select a product that benefits/uses the chosen theme
effectively.
3. For the chosen product, they should prepare a short presentation in the form of video,

5. Teams will be judged based on the choice and feasibility of product, taglines and

4. Participants can make use of posters, ppts, templates etc. to aid them pitch their
product.
5. At the end of the pitch, there will be questions from the judges that will have to be
addressed by the participants.
6. Teams will be judged on market analysis, finances, product/service analysis, marketing
strategy, competition, investment proposal (how they convince investors to invest on
their product) and overall presentation skills.

4. If any participant is found to engage in foul play, the entire team will be immediately
disqualified.

• Robotics

• Cybersecurity and its risk assessment techniques
• Optical Communication and Networks
• Emerging trends in Photonics
• MEMS
• Antenna Systems and its Applications
• Signal and Image Processing
• Machine Learning/ AI for networks

EVENT RULES:
• Only a maximum of 4 authors and only student authors allowed. It is not necessary that all authors must be from same institute.
• Plagiarism is a very serious consideration. Only original research works accepted.
• The paper must not have been presented and published elsewhere.
• The paper must be in two columns IEEE format. This format must be strictly adhered to. The paper length must be limited to 6 pages.
• The paper must be in .pdf format.
• Shortlisted papers will be called for presentation.
• The paper must be sent through mail. The instructions are as follows
• The subject must be the title of the paper
• The body of the email should include- Name and Institute of participant (from first author till the fourth one), Department and Year, Contact numbers, Email addresses.
• paper as an attachment.


EVENT DESCRIPTION:
Round 1:
• All the entries from various colleges will be evaluated and shortlisted by the chair and
secretary.
• Authors of shortlisted papers will be notified for the next round.
Round 2:
• The selected participants will be provided with a google meet/ zoom link. They will be requested to login atleast 5 minutes before the start of the event.
• All the participants except the speaker will be requested to mute their audio and video.
• Every participant is required to ensure stable internet connectivity throughout the event.
• In case of internet instability, one can leave the meet and re-join, but presence of first author is must for the entire stretch of the event.
• They will be explained about the event, panel of judges, judging criteria, and prizes.
• The first author will be then intimated to submit a video recording of their
presentation time limited to 5 minutes. They will be provided an hour to submit their presentation.
• The panel will then evaluate the presentation. During the evaluation of presentation, the participants and the panel will be provided a zoom/google meet link. Once the panel finishes the evaluation of presentation, they can immediately shoot their questions to the authors of the corresponding presentation and assess them.
• Evaluations of Round 1 and Round 2 will be consolidated.
• The team with the highest score will be declared as the “Best StudentPresentation”.
All participants shortlisted for Round 2 will be given participation certificates.



EVALUATION CRITERIA:
Round 1:
1. Each submission will be reviewed by the Chair and Secretary.
2. Applications will be judged for completeness, the author’s ability to follow
instructions, background and significance, research design, organization,
writing/format and overall quality.
Round 2:
1. Content of presentation:
This category assesses the proper use of graphics and text to effectively
communicate the point and generate interest in the audience.
2. Background, originality and significance:
Judges will consider the following questions: Does the study show originality or creativity in
research design and/or interpretation? Does it address a new problem, or an old problem in a
new way? Are the study and its results important? Do they shed new light on the issue at hand,
or suggest important new methods, procedures, etc.? Does the presentation make a clear case for
how the research fits into a larger context?
3. Research Design:
This category refers primarily, but not exclusively, to methods of data
collection and analysis.
4. Organisation and delivery of content:
This category will look into the logical flow of ideas, cohesion and its
effective use in the allotted time frame.
5. Visual Aids and Technical aspects:
This category will assess the use of visual aids and technical aspects used in the
presentation.
6. Ability to field questions:
This category will assess the ability to answer the questions accurately without
paraphrasing or repeating the question thereby indicating the depth of knowledge in
the work submitted.
The panel’s decision will be the final.
